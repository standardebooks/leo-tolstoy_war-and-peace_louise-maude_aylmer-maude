The Maudes in their translation anglicized several of the principal characters given names, and almost completely eliminated the use of Russian patronymics and dimunitives throughout. Those have been restored in this production. The exception is where the given names are in French in the original; I left those intact, and converted to the French spelling where it was different than what the Maudes used, to make it clear that it was French being spoken.

- In many cases, the Russian version of the names were used elsewhere in the novel. (The Maudes were very inconsistent in their anglicization, with the exception of the five families.) In those cases, I used their version of the name.

- If the names weren’t present elsewhere in Maude, I used a scan of Garnett's translation to guide me. I then standardized the spelling based on how Maude transliterated their names.

- The Maude’s use Bonaparte throughout, but Tolstoy used both Bonaparte and Buonaparte, and there are multiple discussions in the book revolving around the difference. I’ve restored Buonaparte where it was used in the original, either in French or in Russian.

- I used Garnett as the starting point for the patronymics and dimunitives, but often had to refer to an original Russian scan to make final determinations.

The Maudes also changed from the original four-book structure to just 15 parts with two epilogues. This restores the original structure.
